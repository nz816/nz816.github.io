Make Your Voting Plan

What is a voting plan? Simply put, it's figuring out how and when you will vote. Making a voting plan is important because research shows that those who make a voting plan are more likely to actually vote.

To make this process easier, use this checklist to help make your voting plan:
üó≥Ô∏è Will you be voting in-person? If yes:
Are you registered to vote?
Have you checked your registration status? (Make sure all information is up-to-date and correct!)
Will you be voting early or on election day?
Do you know where your poll site is?
Do you know the hours of your poll site?
What time will you be voting?
How will you get to your poll site?
Do you need ID to vote? (You may be required to show proof of identification if you did not provide either a state-issued ID number or the last four digits of your Social Security Number when you registered to vote.)
